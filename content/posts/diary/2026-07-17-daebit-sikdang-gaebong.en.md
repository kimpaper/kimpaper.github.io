Korean BBQ night at Daebit Sikdang near home. Their thing is **kkot-samgyeop**
(꽃삼겹, "flower pork belly") grilled on a dome-shaped stone pan.

![Kkot-samgyeop](/images/2026-07-17-daebit-2.jpg)

Kkot-samgyeop is thin-sliced pork belly rolled up like a rose. It goes on the
hot stone dome together with kimchi and mountain vegetables — gosari (fernbrake)
and minari (water dropwort).

![On the grill](/images/2026-07-17-daebit-1.jpg)

The dome shape drains the fat away as it cooks, so the pork crisps up without
swimming in grease. Wrap it with the greens (**ssam** style) and it never gets
heavy. There's a little pot of sauce bubbling in the center for dipping.

![Self-serve bar](/images/2026-07-17-daebit-3.jpg)

Self-serve bar for garlic, ssamjang (dipping paste), peppers, and pickled
onions — refill as much as you want, no waving anyone down.

![Nurungji-tang](/images/2026-07-17-daebit-4.jpg)

They finish you off with a free **nurungji-tang** (누룽지탕) — a mild, toasty
scorched-rice soup. The classic gentle landing after a spicy, smoky meal.

## Where

- Daebit Sikdang — 26 Gocheok-ro 30-gil, Guro-gu, Seoul (near Gaebong Stn.)
