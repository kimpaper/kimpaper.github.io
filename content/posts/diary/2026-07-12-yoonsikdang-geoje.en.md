Lunch on Geoje Island at a place called Yoonsikdang. We ordered hanchi mulhoe
and a rock-octopus rice bowl.

## Hanchi mulhoe

![Hanchi mulhoe](/images/2026-07-12-yoonsikdang-2.jpg)

**Mulhoe** (물회) is one of Korea's great summer seafood dishes: raw seafood
tossed in a sweet-tangy gochujang dressing, then submerged in icy broth —
basically a cold, drinkable raw-fish salad. **Hanchi** (한치) is a small squid
prized for its tender crunch. On a hot day this is hard to beat.

## Dolmuneo-deopbap (rock octopus rice bowl)

![Rock octopus rice bowl](/images/2026-07-12-yoonsikdang-1.jpg)

Chunks of rock octopus stir-fried in a sweet-spicy sauce, served over rice
with shredded cabbage, perilla leaves, and gim (seaweed). The octopus is thick
and springy.

![All mixed up](/images/2026-07-12-yoonsikdang-3.jpg)

You mix everything together — that's the way. The sauce coats every grain of
rice and the spoon doesn't stop.
