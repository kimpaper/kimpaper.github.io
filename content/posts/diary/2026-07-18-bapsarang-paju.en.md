Lunch at Bapsarang, a countryside diner in Paju (north of Seoul), the kind of
place Koreans call a **baekban-jip** (백반집).

![The full spread](/images/2026-07-18-bapsarang-1.jpg)

**What is baekban?** Baekban (백반) is Korea's everyday set meal: rice, a stew,
a main, and a spread of **banchan** (반찬, shared side dishes) — all included,
no extra charge, refills welcome. It's home cooking sold cheap.

![Banchan](/images/2026-07-18-bapsarang-3.jpg)

The banchan is the soul of a baekban place: stir-fried dried shrimp, acorn
jelly with quail eggs, seasoned burdock and greens, fresh kimchi. You graze
across everything and suddenly your rice bowl is empty.

![Menu board](/images/2026-07-18-bapsarang-4.jpg)

The baekban set is ₩9,000 (~$6.5). They also do spicy pork, squid stir-fry,
braised pollack — and breakfast, and delivery. Countryside diner value.
