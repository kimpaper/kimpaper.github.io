Late-night eating is practically a national sport in Korea — it even has its
own word, **yasik** (야식). Tonight's yasik came by delivery app.

![Sora-muchim](/images/2026-07-18-yasik-1.jpg)

**Sora-muchim** (소라무침) — sea snail tossed with minari and perilla leaves in
a sweet-tangy gochujang dressing. Chewy, spicy, bright. I boiled a batch of
**somyeon** (소면, thin wheat noodles) myself to go with it: mixing the noodles
into the leftover dressing is honestly the main event.

![Ojingeo-sukhoe](/images/2026-07-18-yasik-2.jpg)

**Ojingeo-sukhoe** (오징어숙회) — squid briefly blanched, served plain with a
chojang (vinegared gochujang) dip. Mild and clean, the perfect counterpart to
the spicy snail salad.

Two delivery dishes plus a pot of noodles and the homesul table is set.
This is why Korean nights are dangerous.
